Scanning a textured surface with a tool generates rich high-frequency vibrations that demonstrate perceptual properties of a surface. To achieve full distal attribution, sensed vibrations must correspond to the user inputs in a physically appropriate manner \cite{Loomis92}.

A reasonable idea is that varying one's exploratory speed and normal force must significantly alter the
realism. However, studies (e.g. \cite{Culbertson15-WHC-Vibrations}) show that removing force responsiveness does not have a significant effect on the perceived  realism, whereas removing speed responsiveness is more salient to users. Therefore, haptic textures in this thesis include speed responsiveness and not force responsiveness.
